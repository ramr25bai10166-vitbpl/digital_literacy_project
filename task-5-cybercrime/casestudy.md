\# Case Study: UPI / Online Fraud in Payment  



In recent times, online payment frauds through UPI have become increasingly prevalent among students and working professionals alike – this refers to fraud attempts in which people are tricked by someone whom they think is genuine into authorising a payment or giving sensitive detail (i.e. OTP, PIN etc).  



Consider a scenario where Rohan, a student, wants to sell his old phone on an online classified. After posting the ad, he receives a message from a so-called “buyer” who he thinks is a genuine purchaser and agrees to immediately pay him. The “buyer” sends him a QR Code and requests that he scans it so he can “receive” money from the buyer’s account. Rohan trusts the process and scans the QR Code, enters his UPI PIN and—much to Rohan’s surprise—he does not receive any money; he has just unknowingly authorised a payment from his own bank account for a considerable sum of money. 



The majority of those who fall victim to this type of online fraud are not fully aware of how UPI works. Although first-time UPI users can become victims, students and other individuals urgently needing money are targeted more than others. Scammers usually exploit the confusion surrounding the terms “requesting” and “sending” money.  



The consequences of this type of fraud can be extremely severe for the individual victim. In seconds, an individual can lose all of their savings, and recapturing the funds is often impossible. In addition to the financial loss, victims may also experience extreme levels of stress and embarrassment, in addition to a lost faith in electronic and digital transactions.  



This case demonstrates a basic but very important rule that you should never have to scan a QR code or enter your PIN in order to receive money. Being aware and double-checking the validity of those requests can help you to avoid the majority of these types of fraud.
