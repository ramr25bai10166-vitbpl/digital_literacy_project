## Goal  
The overall goal of my Digital Literacy Project is to demonstrate what I've learned from my studies in Digital Literacy and how these skills can be applied in different ways. 

## Summary  
My Digital Literacy Project has five main components that connect the theories and principles learned through my Digital Literacy Course to my actual use of that knowledge within a digital landscape.  
**Task1**: I first created a Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic using Canva, visually breaking down the complexities of various topics associated with digital literacy such as finding information efficiently, and staying safe while using the Internet.  
**Task2**: In order to assist me in my future as a CSE major concentrating in AI and ML, I also created a Digital Portfolio that provides examples of work from platforms such as Kaggle for hands-on work with data science, Stack Overflow for troubleshooting technical issues, and ResearchGate for collaborating on academic research. 
**Task3**: Besides developing my digital portfolio I also have experientially used coding through HackerRank and made a Google Forms digital literacy awareness quiz to test the awareness of online safety and digital skills. You can take my quiz by clicking on: https://forms.gle/suxxdn2twxn1EXEb6  
**Task4**: In addition to these components, I have created a professional email response and a social media etiquette guideline that has made it clear to me that improper use of digital communication can have a very negative effect on the dynamics of a group.  
**Task5**: Lastly, I developed a new way of thinking about cybercrime by completing a Cybercrime Case Study of UPI fraud. This has taught me how quickly cyber criminals can wing it online and given me a "hard and fast" rule to always avoid inputting your PIN when receiving funds and treat any unsolicited QR codes as a very serious red flag.
