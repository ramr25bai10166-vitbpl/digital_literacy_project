\# Social Media Checklist for College Students



\## The Do’s



\*\*Check your privacy settings once in a while\*\*

Apps change things all the time. Something you thought was private last month might not be anymore, so it’s worth a quick check.



\*\*Pause before you post\*\*

Use the “billboard test”: if you’d feel weird seeing it on a giant screen in front of your parents or professor, maybe don’t post it.



\*\*Use social media to build connections\*\*

Follow people in your field, seniors, or alumni. Even casually engaging now can help a lot when you’re looking for internships or jobs later.



\*\*Give credit\*\*

If you are Sharing something cool, tag the person who made it. It’s respectful and keeps things geniune.



\*\*Double-check before sharing news\*\*

Not everything online is true. A quick search can save you from spreading something misleading.



\---



\## The Don’ts



\*\*Don’t post when you’re not thinking clearly\*\*

Late-night or party posts can feel fun in the moment, but they can stick around longer than you expect.



\*\*Don’t argue with strangers online\*\*

If someone’s clearly trying to provoke you, it’s not worth it. Walking away always looks better than losing your cool.



\*\*Don’t rant about professors or bosses online\*\*

Even private stories or acounts are not really private. Screenshots exist, so keep serious venting offline.



\*\*Don’t share your live location\*\*

Posting exactly where you are (like your dorm or class) in real time isn’t the safest. It’s better to post after you have left.



\*\*Don’t compare your life to others posts\*\*

Most people only show their best moments. Don’t let someone else’s “perfect” feed make you feel like you are behind.
